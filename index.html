  • Everyday

    Graduation Project at Pace University

    Everyday is the app I created for Google Glass as my graduation project. It used the Google Mirror API and it was completely a web based app that sent notifications for users with Google Glass if there was a holiday every day. The user could specify the time for each notification and the card sent allowed people to share the holiday through Twitter app. Created in Java, and using MySQL as the primary database it was hosted in Amazon’s AWS. Currently, the app is not live due to the dwindling use of Google Glass, but the entire code is open source and can be seen on Github.

  • ParentGuard

    Windows Phone Mobile App

    In a Microsoft Women’s Hackathon at Pace, Nivedita Joshi, Patrice Minott and I developed a Windows Phone 8 app in C# that tracks teens driving. The worries of parents when their teens are driving are really high nowadays and this app allows the phone to track the speed, time of arrival and geolocation of the driver and sends an SMS to parents’ or guardians’ phone for safe measure.

Passions

I’m absolutely passionate about developing software. I love delivering high quality products and the whole creative process of making it. I consider myself a very happy and active person who likes to say yes to new projects and experiences. Nothing can replace the thrill to learn something new or work on a new project.

My passion for coding started out very early and without realizing that I liked it as much as I do. When I was around 12 years old, I used to spend a lot of time on the Nickelodeon forums for shows like “Hey Arnold!” or “Rugrats”. The forums allowed the person to create customizable names using CSS and HTML. It amazed me how regular text could be changed into a “Word Art” (yeah, we used those all the time) with a few lines of code. But it wasn’t until I was a senior in high school that I decided to pursue my passion for computing at UNITEC with a Computer Systems Engineering Degree.
